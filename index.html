In the religion Hinduism, Sita was the daughter of Janaka and wife of Rama, an avatar of god Vishnu. The word Sita means “furrow”, that is, the line created on the farm when it is ploughed. There is a story about her birth. Her father Janaka was ploughing the land and a furrow was formed. From that furrow a baby girl came out. Janaka took the girl as his daughter and named her Sita.

In Hinduism, Sita is considered a form of goddess Lakshmi. She is an incarnation of goddess Lakshmi. Sita is worshiped for her greatness and for her loyalty.she was the goddess of loyalty and her responsibility.
